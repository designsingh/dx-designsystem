Two connected problems: (1) Archetypes need to graduate from DLS conference cards into a full DesignX platform product — like Salary or Job Board. (2) DLS and DesignX need clear rules for where they stay separate, where they merge, and how content flows between them.

The Two Deliverables
① Archetypes as Product
Currently: DLS conference lanyard cards with 5 types.

Becomes: A full product in the DesignX tool stack. Assessment quiz → result reveal → profile badge → content recommendations → team matching. Lives alongside Salary and Job Board as a core platform tool.

Gets its own color world in the DesignX system (gold/amber family), plus each archetype keeps its individual color for the light/editorial context.
② Brand Boundary Map
Problem: DLS talks get recorded and live on app.designx. DesignX events also produce talks. Archetypes born at DLS now live on DesignX. Where does one brand end and the other begin?

Solution: A systematic map of every content type — where it originates, where it lives, what brand it wears, and how it transitions when it crosses the boundary.

BRAND
BOUNDARY MAP

DLS and DesignX are two brands under one roof. DLS is the premium editorial experience. DesignX is the platform utility. Some things stay separate. Some merge. Some cross-pollinate. Here's the systematic map.

The Three Zones
🌤 DLS Territory
Owns: conference brand, speaker relationships, editorial voice, Bauhaus visual language, ticket sales, physical collateral, designleadership.io

Brand: maximalist collage, Instrument Serif headlines, multi-color palette, paper grain, Bauhaus blocks
⚡ Shared Territory
Both claim: talks library, archetype assessment, speaker profiles, community events, content recommendations

Rule: content wears the brand of where it's displayed. A DLS talk on app.designx wears dark. The same talk on designleadership.io wears light.
🌙 DesignX Territory
Owns: platform tools (Salary, Jobs, The Stack), community membership, user profiles, Slack, newsletter, app.designx.community

Brand: minimal dark system, DM Sans headlines, purple gradient, glass effects, systematic grid

The Golden Rule
Content wears the brand of where it's displayed.
A DLS talk recording doesn't stay "DLS branded" forever. When it lives on designleadership.io, it wears light Bauhaus. When it lives on app.designx.community, it wears dark platform. The content is the same — the presentation layer changes.

Think of it like water taking the shape of its container. The talk doesn't change. The brand wrapper does.

Exception: DLS speaker announcement cards are ALWAYS light Bauhaus, even when shared on dark-mode platforms (YouTube, newsletter). The Bauhaus card IS the content — it's a visual asset, not a text post wearing a wrapper.
