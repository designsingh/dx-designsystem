THE BAUHAUS
COLLAGE SYSTEM

The geometric block collage is the single most distinctive visual element of DLS. Overlapping rectangles and circles in saturated colors, layered with grain, creating a risograph/screen-print texture. They appear behind speakers, around logos, and scattered across compositions.

Block Construction — How They Layer
4 LAYERS: BACK → MID → CIRCLES → ACCENTS
Anatomy: Isolated Blocks
Rectangles at 0° (never rotated). Circles for warmth. Overlap creates color mixing where grain adds texture. 4 depth layers, each slightly lower opacity.

Block Color Rules
Minimum colors per collage4 distinct hues
Maximum colors per collage7–8 (beyond looks muddy)
Must includeAlways 1 warm (red/orange/yellow) + 1 cool (blue/green)
Block shapesRectangles (0° rotation) + circles. Never triangles.
Opacity range0.6 – 0.95 (never fully opaque, grain shows through)
OverlapRequired. 30-50% of blocks must overlap another.
Grain on topAlways. Film grain at 35-45% covers entire composition.
Block count6-12 elements per speaker card. 3-6 for smaller uses.

WHERE LIGHT
MEETS DARK

DLS light is a maximalist Bauhaus collage. DesignX dark is a minimal system UI. They share purple as the connector, and halftone as a photographic treatment. Everything else changes at the boundary.

What Transfers Across Modes
Shared DNA (both modes):
Purple as one accent color#6D28D9 on light · #8B5CF6 on dark
Halftone portrait treatmentB&W grayscale + dot pattern overlay
Instrument Serif presenceProminent on light · restricted on dark
DM Sans for body/UISame weight hierarchy both modes
JetBrains Mono for dataDates, stats, prices, meta
Film/paper grainLight: 35-45% heavy · Dark: 15-20% subtle
