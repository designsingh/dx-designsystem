Logo Usage
DesignX and DLS are separate brand marks that share a family resemblance through weight and geometry. Each mark has specific rules for placement, spacing, and background treatment.
DesignX
On Dark
DesignX Wordmark
Primary platform mark. White text with mint "X" accent on black or near-black backgrounds. Use DM Sans 900 uppercase. Never on light surfaces.
DLS'26
On Light
DLS Conference Mark
Conference mark on cream or white surfaces only. DM Sans 900 with Instrument Serif italic year. Purple accent for the edition number.
Clear Space & Sizing
• Minimum clear space: the height of the "X" on all sides
• Minimum width: 120px on screen, 30mm in print
• The DesignX mark should always be the most prominent brand element on dark surfaces
• DLS mark should be the most prominent on light/conference materials
• Both marks should be horizontally or vertically centered in their container
Never Do This
• Never place the DesignX wordmark on light or cream backgrounds
• Never place the DLS mark on dark backgrounds
• Never rotate, distort, or apply effects to either mark
• Never change the typefaces (no substituting fonts)
• Never use the mint "X" color for the DLS mark or purple for DesignX
• Never add drop shadows, outlines, or gradients to the wordmark

When to Use Instrument Serif
Instrument Serif is a precision tool, not a workhorse. It creates moments of contrast and elegance within the DM Sans system. Always italic. Never in body text.

Photography & Illustration Direction
Photography is how the brand connects to real people. Every image should feel like you're witnessing a genuine moment in the design leadership world — not watching a stock photo shoot.
Subject Matter
Platform & Tools
• Designers at work: screens, whiteboards, sketches
• Close-ups of hands on keyboards or drawing tablets
• Workspaces and studio environments
• Abstract textures and materials (paper, fabric, concrete)
Community & Meetups
• Candid group conversations at dinners and events
• Warm, ambient environments: restaurants, rooftops
• Laughter, gestures, eye contact between people
• Table settings, name cards, event details
DLS Conference
• Speakers on stage: dramatic lighting, mid-gesture
• Audience reactions: listening, note-taking, applauding
• Venue architecture and signage
• Behind-the-scenes preparation moments
Treatment & Cropping
Do
• Shoot candid — real moments, not posed portraits
• Favor warm, natural lighting over harsh flash
• Crop tight on faces and hands for speaker cards
• Convert to grayscale + halftone for dark-mode treatments
• Apply 15-38% film grain to match the surface mode
• Use shallow depth of field for intimate community shots
• Desaturate slightly for dark surfaces, keep saturation for light
Don't
• No stock photography with obvious artificial staging
• No cheesy "teamwork" cliches (hands stacked, high fives)
• No heavy color grading that makes skin tones unnatural
• No full-color photos on dark backgrounds (desaturate first)
• No wide-angle event shots with no focal subject
• No illustrations that mimic a cartoon or children's-book style
• No AI-generated portraits or people
Illustration Style
When photography is not available or appropriate, illustration follows a geometric, Bauhaus-informed style on light surfaces, and a minimal, line-based style on dark surfaces.
Light Surfaces (DLS, Editorial)
• Bold geometric shapes from the Bauhaus palette
• Paper-textured, risograph-inspired prints
• Overlapping blocks with 0.6–0.95 opacity
• No outlines — shapes are always filled
• Subject matter: abstract compositions, not literal objects
Dark Surfaces (Platform, Tools)
• Thin, monochrome line art at 40% opacity
• Data-visualization-inspired forms (grids, nodes, arcs)
• Mint accent on a single element for focal point
• No filled shapes — always outlines or dots
• Subject matter: systems, networks, abstract structure
Texture System
Texture is what separates DesignX from flat digital brands. Film grain, halftone dots, paper grain, and glass effects create tactile depth across all three surface modes.
Film Grain 15%
Dark mode default
Film Grain 38%
DLS / Light mode
Halftone Dots
4px grid · multiply
Paper + Grain
Risograph feel
Do
• Use 15% grain on dark backgrounds for subtle texture
• Use 35-45% grain on light/cream DLS surfaces
• Layer halftone over portraits (grayscale + multiply)
• Use paper grain on DLS Bauhaus cards for risograph feel
• Keep glass effects at 92% opacity with 24px blur
Don't
• Don't use heavy grain (35%+) on dark surfaces — too noisy
• Don't use halftone dots on light/cream surfaces without Bauhaus blocks
• Don't skip grain entirely — flat digital surfaces feel off-brand
• Don't use paper texture on dark mode — it reads as dirty, not tactile
• Don't combine halftone + paper grain on the same element
