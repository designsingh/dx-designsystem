The Golden Rule
Content wears the brand of where it's displayed.
A DLS talk recording doesn't stay "DLS branded" forever. When it lives on designleadership.io, it wears light Bauhaus. When it lives on app.designx.community, it wears dark platform. The content is the same — the presentation layer changes.

Think of it like water taking the shape of its container. The talk doesn't change. The brand wrapper does.

Exception: DLS speaker announcement cards are ALWAYS light Bauhaus, even when shared on dark-mode platforms. The Bauhaus card IS the content — it's a visual asset, not a text post wearing a wrapper.
Platform Accent
The DesignX platform accent is green — it signals "you're on DesignX." It sits above the 6 color worlds, which signal content type. Green appears as light, not paint: glows, ambient luminance, subtle borders. Never flat fills on large surfaces.

Platform Green vs. Salary Green
Platform Mint#2DD47A — cool, cyan-shifted. Ambient glows, UI chrome.
Salary Neon#AAFF00 — warm, yellow-shifted. Data visualisation, salary figures.
They coexist because they serve different roles. Platform green is the shell; Salary green is the content. One is UI chrome, the other is data.

Color Worlds ≠ Brand Identity
Color worlds are how the DesignX dark platform categorises content. They are NOT the brand identity of the content itself.
DLS on designleadership.ioCream / Bauhaus / Serif — DLS's own brand. No purple.
DLS content on app.designxPurple glow/gradient — the platform's category signal for conference content.
The purple isn't DLS's identity. It's the DesignX platform saying "this content is conference-related." Same logic applies to all 6 worlds — Salary Neon isn't the Salary tool's brand, it's how salary content is categorised on the dark platform.
