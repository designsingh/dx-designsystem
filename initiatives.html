Gradient Identity — Mesh Variants
All 6 color worlds rendered as mesh gradients (layered radials) with different texture treatments. Top row: standard intensity for ambient use. Bottom row: bright/saturated for hero moments.
Standard Intensity
Mesh — Clean
6 radial layers · no texture overlay
Pure mesh. Soft organic color pools on black. Loading states, ambient backgrounds, section separators.
Mesh — Film Grain
feTurbulence noise · 20% overlay
Fractal noise softens the digital mesh. Organic, editorial warmth. Brand stories, about pages, dark backgrounds.
Mesh — Halftone
5px dot grid · overlay blend
Print-inspired dots over mesh. Retro-editorial texture. Newsletter headers, conference posters, social art.
Bright / Saturated
Bright — Stipple
High sat mesh · dual micro-dot dither
Pumped saturation with fine stipple texture. Lo-fi meets high-energy. Hero banners, email headers, data viz overlays.
Bright — Frosted
High sat mesh · backdrop-filter blur(40px)
Bright color pools diffused through glass blur. Premium aurora glow. Splash screens, feature drops, modal overlays.
Bright — Scanline
High sat mesh · 3px horizontal scan
CRT-inspired scanlines over saturated mesh. Digital artifact energy. Social cards, announcement heroes, video thumbnails.
When to use which
StandardAmbient, supporting contexts — backgrounds, loading, section dividers
BrightHero, primary contexts — announcements, headers, splash screens
CleanMaximum color clarity. No texture competing with content on top.
GrainOrganic warmth. Best for large surfaces where you want softness.
HalftonePrint energy. Works best at medium sizes — cards, headers.
StippleDigital texture. Fine enough to not distract from overlaid text.
FrostedPremium diffusion. Best for overlays where you need depth + legibility.
ScanlineMaximum energy. Digital artifact vibe. Social-first moments.

The Golden Rule
Purple is the DesignX platform's category signal for conference content. It is NOT DLS's brand color. DLS's own brand is cream/Bauhaus/serif on designleadership.io. The one exception: speaker cards with Bauhaus blocks are always light — they ARE the content, not a platform wrapper.
